Ikigai

By Francesc Miralles and Hector Garcia

The people of Japan believe that everyone has an ikigai – a reason to jump out of bed each morning.

And according to the residents of the Japanese island of Okinawa – the world’s longest-living people – finding it is the key to a longer and more fulfilled life.

The above given image explains that what you love and what you are good at, becomes your passion

What you love and what you can be paid for becomes your profession

What you can be paid for and what the world needs becomes your vocation

What the world needs and what you love becomes your mission

Together your passion, profession, vocation and mission make up your ikigai

The ten rules of ikigai are-

1) Stay active, don't retire-Keep doing what you love

2) Take it slow- When you leave urgency behind, life and time take a new meaning

3) Dont't fill your stomach- Less is more when it comes to a long life. Eat a little less than your hunger demands.

4) Surround yourself with good friends- Friends are the best medicine, there for confiding, sharing stories, getting advice, having fun, dreaming, in other words for living.

5) Get in shape for your next birthday- The body you move through life needs a bit of gentle daily maintenance.

6) Smile- It's a priviledge to be here and now- in a world full of so many possibilites. So, smile.

7) Reconnect with nature- Human beings are made to be a part of the natural world. Return to it as often as you can.

8) Give thanks- Give thanks for everything that brightens your days and makes you feel lucky to be alive.

9) Live in the moment- Today is all we have. Make it worth remembering.

10) Find your ikigai
